\section*{Abstract}
The aim of this report is to characterize Gaussian beams. Firstly, the beam diameter of the laser is recorded at different distances with and without a lens, allowing us to determine the beam waist by comparing with theory. We determine the beam waist to be $W_0 = 224(4) \unit{\micro\m}$. The second part of the experiment includes an optical cavity, allowing us to study higher modes of the laser. We can qualitatively identify TEM$_{01}$, TEM$_{10}$ and TEM$_{02}$ modes using a CCD camera. We also recorded a spectrum of higher order modes and determined the frequency separation. We can explain the measured mode spacing by assuming contributions to the spectrum coming exclusively from TEM$_{mn}$ modes with $m + n = 2$.
